ABOUT SEOUL

Seoul, the capital of South Korea, is a dynamic city blending centuries-old traditions with cutting-edge technology. From historic palaces to towering skyscrapers, Seoul offers an unforgettable journey through culture, cuisine, and creativity.

From the majestic Gyeongbokgung Palace to the soaring Lotte World Tower, Seoul seamlessly blends centuries-old architecture with cutting-edge design. Wander through Bukchon Hanok Village to experience traditional Korean homes, or explore the neon-lit stree

Seoul is the heartbeat of K-pop, Korean drama, and global fashion trends. Yet its soul remains rooted in customs like wearing hanbok, celebrating Chuseok, and savoring tea ceremonies. Whether you're dancing in Hongdae or meditating in a temple, the city invites you to embrace both the old and the new.

Food lovers rejoice—Seoul is a paradise of flavors. Indulge in sizzling samgyeopsal, spicy tteokbokki, and comforting bibimbap. Street markets like Gwangjang and Myeongdong serve up unforgettable bites, while upscale districts offer refined Korean cuisine.

Whether you're drawn by history, inspired by pop culture, or simply hungry for adventure, Seoul welcomes you with open arms and endless possibilities.

Foods of Seoul

Taste the vibrant flavors of Seoul — from street snacks to traditional Korean meals.

Kimchi

Spicy, fermented cabbage — a classic Korean side dish loved by all.

Bibimbap

A rice bowl with veggies, meat, egg, and chili paste — colorful and nutritious.

Tteokbokki

Soft rice cakes simmered in a spicy red sauce — a must-try street food.

Samgyeopsal

Grilled pork belly slices served with lettuce wraps and dipping sauces.

CULTURE

From the colorful hanbok to the lively K-pop scene, Seoul’s culture is a perfect mix of old and new.

Traditional Hanbok

The Hanbok (한복) is the traditional attire of Korea, worn for centuries and still cherished today for ceremonies, holidays, and cultural events. The word hanbok literally means “Korean clothing.”

K-pop and Entertainment

Seoul is the global epicenter of K-pop and Korean entertainment, offering fans immersive experiences from concerts and music shows to themed cafes and shopping.

Authentic Korean Food

Authentic Korean cuisine is built on balance and harmony, often combining spicy, sour, salty, and umami flavors in a single meal.
